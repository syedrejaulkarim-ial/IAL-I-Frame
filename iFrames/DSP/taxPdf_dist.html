RGESS REPORT
 
 
 
Everyone is allowed to invest in RGESS. However investors must meet certain criteria to avail a tax benifit on RGESS. The eligibility criteria is listed as under:
If you already have a demat account:
If you have not invested in securities or derivatives before Nov 23, 2012, you can invest in these schemes through your demat account to get tax benefits, with 2 additional criteria to be met. You have to:
1. Be a resident Indian.
2. Have a gross annual income from all sources less than/equal to Rs 12 lacs Note: You can get tax benefits by investing in a RGESS compliant MF scheme only by investing through your demat account.
If you don’t have a demat account:
You will need to open one before you invest, with 2 additional criteria to be met. You have to:
1. Be a resident Indian.
2. Have a gross annual income from all sources less than/equal to Rs 12 lacs Note: Even you have held stocks in a physical certificate format, you will still be eligible for tax saving if you start a new demat account to invest in these schemes.
